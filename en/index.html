EXAMPLES

Sample dashboards

Selected views show both clear control interfaces and the operating logic of the installation.

Heating control

The dashboard allows managing heating from multiple sources, such as off-grid photovoltaics with a buffer tank and a gas boiler. It enables changing operating modes, temperatures, and setback thresholds, and automates energy savings when the house is empty or in vacation mode. The entire setup also monitors the system's logic so that heat sources do not operate simultaneously in an incorrect manner.

Buffer, process, and heat source dependencies

The view shows the buffer status and the operating logic of the entire heating system. It makes it easy to assess the temperature in the upper and lower parts of the buffer, informs whether energy is being used for central heating, and whether the water is at the right temperature for domestic hot water.

Blinds, groups, and scenes

The dashboard allows you to control individual blinds, groups of blinds, and home scenes. It also facilitates managing the entire home and combines manual control with automation based on the time of day, presence, and selected events in the system.

Device integrations

The dashboard allows convenient control of the robot vacuum: starting a full house clean or just selected zones, pausing work, and sending the device back to the dock. It also shows the vacuum's status and lets you adjust the cleaning power.

Heat recovery and ventilation

The view allows switching between manual mode and time-based or condition-based automation. It lets you control the recovery speed, airing out, and operating logic without needing to access system settings.

Lighting and glass panels

The dashboard replicates the physical glass switches in the home and shows the current lighting status in individual rooms. It facilitates quick light control, organizes the room layout, and provides a clear overview of what is currently turned on.

Network stability

Zigbee topology and connection diagnostics

The dashboard shows how the Zigbee network is built: which devices connect directly to the coordinator, which work through routers, and the connection quality across the entire topology.

  • network topology and routing paths
  • connection quality and LQI values
  • relationships between the coordinator, routers, and end devices
